A week into running dadeog.neocities.org and the experiment is teaching me more about taste than I expected.

The pitch was nostalgia and a technical flex. The reality is that building a personal site with no framework, no build step, and no design system forces you to make every layout decision on purpose. There is no Tailwind class to hide behind. There is no component library imposing a grid. You write a CSS rule, you reload the page, you decide if you like it. The feedback loop is brutal and honest.

This connects back to the studio in a way I did not plan. Most of what I make professionally lives inside someone else's design system or somebody else's third-party platform. Neocities is the opposite. Pure files, pure control, pure consequences. It is the closest thing to writing in a sketchbook that the modern web allows.

The lesson I am stealing for client work: constraints sharpen taste faster than freedom does. A site with no framework forces you to know why every pixel is where it is. Real design happens in those small decisions, not in the macro layout.

More shrines and arcade pages in the queue. The whole thing stays intentionally unfinished.
