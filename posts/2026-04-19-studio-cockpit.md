Spent the week building the workspace I should have built a year ago. One source of truth for clients, projects, leads, and the calendar. No more flipping between five tools to answer one question.

The structure, kept boring on purpose:

- A pipeline view for prospects, with clear stages and one next action per row.
- A client roster with deal terms, current status, and last touch.
- A projects board for everything in flight, grouped by which property it belongs to.
- A weekly review template that pulls from all of the above and forces a Friday triage.

Why now: the studio is starting to have enough surface area that mental tracking is actively losing money. A lead I forget to follow up on is more expensive than any tool I could buy to remember it for me. The point of a CRM at this scale is not pipeline forecasting. It is making sure nothing falls through cracks I cannot afford.

Tool choice was deliberately unsexy. The best CRM is the one you actually open every morning. Mine opens on the same screen as my calendar so I have no excuse.

The operating system of a studio is the boring thing that makes everything else possible.
