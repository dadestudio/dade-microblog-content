Spent a chunk of last week pulling every Perplexity thread I have ever started into one searchable archive. Final count: 1,328 threads, organized into something I can actually use instead of a blob of forgotten queries.

Why this matters to me: Perplexity is where most of my real research lives. Comparison shopping, technical sanity checks, sourcing claims, half-finished investigations I meant to come back to. The native UI is fine for the moment of asking and terrible at month-six retrieval. You know you asked it something useful in February and the search bar is not going to find it.

The archive now buckets everything by topic, gives me proper full-text search, and surfaces the threads I never finished. The best surprise was finding research from months ago that solved a problem I had started solving from scratch this week. That alone paid for the build.

What I am doing with it next: wiring it into my actual workflow so old research shows up when I need it, instead of dying in a tab I closed. A small studio runs on what it can find again later.

If a tool you use daily does not have a good "what did I do last quarter" view, build the archive yourself. Always worth it.
