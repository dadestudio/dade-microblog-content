The first version of dade.micro.blog is live. This is where everything I ship from the studio gets a public note attached to it. Wins, breakage, weird side projects, the small decisions nobody asks about until later.

Why a dedicated microblog instead of just posting to social: I want a permanent feed I own, with proper RSS, that does not depend on whoever is buying which platform this quarter. Micro.blog gives me a Git-backed posting flow, my own domain, and a clean reading experience without ads or algorithmic shuffling. Posts go in a markdown repo, which means the workflow is the same one I already use for code.

What I plan to write here: short field notes from the studio. The kind of stuff that does not warrant a full essay but is worth marking down. Project launches, quick lessons, things I broke, things I fixed. Categorized so anyone who only cares about one thread can follow just that thread.

Full firehose lives at the RSS feed. If you only care about one project, the BiP-Studio and BiP-MaaS categories give you the slices.
