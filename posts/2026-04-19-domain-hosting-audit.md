Spent a quiet morning going through every domain and hosting bill attached to the studio. Three properties under the microscope: dade.studio, contentcrib.studio, and the merch storefront. Goal: know exactly what I own, where it points, who is hosting it, what it costs, and whether each line item still earns its rent.

Findings, in plain order:

- A few domains pointed at nothing. Either redirected to the right home or queued for letting expire.
- Two registrars when one would be cleaner. Consolidation in progress.
- A hosting plan I forgot existed, billing quietly for over a year. Cancelled.
- DNS spread across providers in a way that made me nervous. Now centralized.

The deeper thing this exposed: you cannot make sharp decisions about a studio you do not have a clear inventory of. I had a vague mental map of what I owned and where it lived. A vague mental map is how you end up paying for ghosts.

The audit doc now lives in my working notes, gets a quarterly review, and any new domain I register goes straight into it the same day. Boring infrastructure work pays for itself the first time you avoid a renewal you did not need.
