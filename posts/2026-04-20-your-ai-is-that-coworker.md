I rewrote the MaaS landing opener this week. The old hook was "Your AI has amnesia." Technically accurate, rhetorically tired.

New wedge: your AI is that coworker. You know the one. Smart and capable, has worked here for six months, and still asks what the company does on Monday. Still asks for your name. Still asks which brand voice to use on a deck they've seen three times.

You wouldn't keep a hire like that. You'd fire them or train them. MaaS is the training path: a portable memory file that rides with you across models, so every session picks up where the last one ended. Claude, GPT, Gemini, Perplexity, all reading from the same source of truth about your business, your voice, your constraints.

The coworker frame lands harder than the amnesia frame for one reason. Amnesia is an abstract clinical word. A coworker who forgets everything is a scene. Scenes sell.

Copy bank is loaded in the CRM. Cold email variants, LinkedIn DMs, reply-to-thread openers, sales deck slide 1 rewritten. The amnesia version is preserved in a toggle for B-testing. We'll know which one actually closes when beta outreach starts.
