Shipped the MaaS landing page this week. Out of my private notes, into a real URL anyone can read.

The feeling is familiar. Every project crosses a threshold the day it stops being a doc you describe to friends and starts being something a stranger can find. The landing page is that threshold. Until this week, MaaS was an idea I had to explain. Now it is a page I can point at.

What is on it: the one-paragraph version of what MaaS is, who it is for, and how to get on the list. Nothing fancy. The job of a landing page at this stage is not to convert. It is to be honest about what the thing is and let the right people self-select.

Live at memory.dade.studio. If the description sounds like a problem you have, the email field is right there.

The hard part of any landing page is not the design. It is being honest about what the product actually is and refusing to oversell it. Got there eventually. More to come as I keep shipping.
